Smart Agriculture (Smart Farming) is a modern farming approach that uses advanced technology to improve productivity, efficiency, and sustainability.
what is smart agriculture ?
Smart agriculture involves using technologies like sensors, automation, and data analysis to monitor and manage farming activities more effectively.
key technologies used :
IoT (Internet of Things): Connects devices like soil sensors and weather stations
Drones: Used for crop monitoring and spraying fertilizers
Artificial Intelligence (AI): Helps predict crop diseases and yields
GPS & Satellite Imaging: Tracks field conditions and crop health
Main features :
Real-time monitoring of soil moisture, temperature, and humidity
Automated irrigation systems
Precision farming (applying water and fertilizers only where needed)
Data-driven decision making
Advantages :
Increases crop yield and quality
Saves water and reduces waste
Reduces manual labor
Helps farmers make better decisions
challenges:
High initial cost
Requires technical knowledge
Dependence on internet connectivity
Future scope :
Smart agriculture will play a major role in feeding the growing population by making farming more efficient and environmentally friendly.
